# TWOS

## Project Brief

TWOS needed to move quickly on both product and brand-facing design. I partnered directly with the CTO to audit the existing mobile onboarding experience, redesign key flows with a more consistent product language, and explore the first iteration of the company’s marketing website.

The work focused on making the app feel clearer, warmer, and easier to enter for two different user states: people joining TWOS for the first time and people arriving through an invitation.

## Role

Contract Product Designer

## Team

CTO, product leadership, engineering

## Company

TWOS

![TWOS UI system and visual language explorations](/projects/twos/twos-ui-system.png)

## Context

TWOS was evolving quickly, and the product needed design support that could keep pace with engineering and strategic decisions. The team had an existing onboarding experience, but the flow needed a clearer hierarchy, a more intentional visual system, and better guidance for new users.

My role was to rapidly translate product direction into screens, flows, and visual systems the team could react to and build from.

## What I worked on

- Audited the current mobile onboarding experience to identify hierarchy, clarity, and interaction issues.
- Redesigned onboarding flows using an updated design-system language.
- Created low-fidelity flow explorations to align on structure before moving into visual polish.
- Brought selected flows into high fidelity for new users and invited users.
- Designed the first iteration of the TWOS marketing website to communicate the product direction externally.

## Onboarding audit

I started by mapping the existing onboarding flow and identifying the moments that created friction: unclear sequencing, inconsistent UI patterns, and screens that asked users to make decisions before the value of the app was fully established.

The audit helped create a shared understanding of what needed to change before moving into new concepts.

![Audit of the existing TWOS onboarding experience](/projects/twos/twos-onboarding-audit.png)

## Flow redesign

After the audit, I explored a revised onboarding structure that introduced the product more clearly and created a smoother path into account creation, permissions, personalization, and the first meaningful product moment.

The goal was not just to make the screens look more polished. It was to make each step feel intentional and reduce the amount of cognitive load placed on a new user.

![Proposed new TWOS onboarding flow](/projects/twos/twos-proposed-onboarding-flow.png)

## Designing for invited and non-invited users

TWOS had to support different entry points into the app. Someone invited by another person needed a different emotional and functional path than someone signing up without an invite.

I created flows that accounted for these different states while keeping the interface language consistent across the product.

![TWOS sign up without invite flow](/projects/twos/twos-sign-up-without-invite.png)

## Visual system direction

Alongside the flow work, I helped push the product toward a warmer and more cohesive interface language. The updated direction used stronger typography, clearer buttons and form states, more consistent spacing, and a visual style that felt more human than purely utility-driven.

This work gave the team a foundation they could continue applying across onboarding, account setup, permissions, and reflection moments inside the app.

## Marketing website

In parallel with the app work, I designed the first iteration of the TWOS marketing site. The goal was to quickly establish a public-facing story for the product and create a visual direction that could connect the brand promise to the mobile app experience.

## Outcome

The work gave TWOS a clearer onboarding direction, a more cohesive visual language, and a stronger foundation for both the mobile product and its first marketing presence. By working directly with the CTO, I was able to move quickly from audit to flow architecture to polished UI while keeping the designs practical for implementation.
